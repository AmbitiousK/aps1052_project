Thank you for your patience in our response. In regards to the questions:
 
1) The BTC Funding rates -- there are actually 2 endpoints to retrieve this from. The Binance is more suited for strategy development or analysis whereas DeriBit is more "live". 
DeriBit endpoint - This interval is very granular and funding rates are updated hourly. 
https://api.cryptodatadownload.com/v1/data/ohlc/deribit/futures/funding/?symbol=BTC-PERPETUAL&limit=25&return=JSON
Binance endpoint - This funding rate is for 8hour intervals, and is on a slight lag. Meaning, we have it through end of June 2025.
https://api.cryptodatadownload.com/v1/data/summary/binance/futures/funding/?symbol=BTCUSDT&limit=25&return=JSON

2) BTC Open interest - There appears to be a gap here. We have Binance 
OI data but is incomplete and stops at end of 2023. We will work to expose
OI data for DeriBit, but that does not appear in the API at this time.

3) BTC Implied Volatility - The implied volatility can be seen in the DeriBit
volatility endpoint. This is "index" implied volatilty, called "DVOL", and is
constructed in such a way as to replicate the equity VIX index, but for crypto. 

https://api.cryptodatadownload.com/v1/data/ohlc/deribit/volatility/?symbol=BTC&limit=25&return=JSON

Historical volatility would need to be calculated via actual price movements in spot, 
which is a derived metric and easily calculated but not exposed in API. 

To your last question, there are no limits to data history. We expose everything that we have in the API.

or backfill.
