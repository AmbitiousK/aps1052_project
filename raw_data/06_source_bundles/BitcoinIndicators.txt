SHORT TERM ON-CHAIN OR CRYPTO-EXCHANGE INDICATORS

We like:
1. funding rate 
Definition	Periodic payment between long and short perpetual futures traders
Directionality	Positive = Longs pay shorts (bullish sentiment); Negative = Shorts pay longs
Signal Type	Sentiment and positioning indicator
Typical Use	Identify overextended leveraged positions (long or short)
Predictive Timeframe	Short-term (<12 hours), decays quickly
Data Sources	Binance, Bybit, CoinGlass, Coinalyze, CryptoQuant, Glassnode
See: 
a. btc_funding_rate.csv and 
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ramkrijal/perpetuals-funding-rates/data?utm_source=chatgpt.com
See funding_rate_sources.docx
b.Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle2\BTC_Binance_FundingRates.csv
c.Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle2\BTC_Deribit_FundingRates.csv

2. hashrate

3. Open Interest (OI) Changes
Definition: Total number of outstanding derivative contracts (futures, options).
Interpretation:
Price ↑ + OI ↑ → Trend continuation likely (new money entering).
Price ↑ + OI ↓ → Short squeeze or covering (less sustainable).
Timeframe: Intraday to multi-day.
Data Source: Binance, Bybit, CoinGlass, CryptoQuant.
See:
a.Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle2\BTC_COT_Report_2025.csv (see  Indicators\COT, weekly)
b.Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle2\BTC_Binance_OITotalsByCallOrPut.csv (options open interest)
c.Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle2\BTC_Binance_FuturesMetrics.csv (futures open interest)

4. Long/Short Ratio
Definition: Ratio of open long vs. short positions.
Interpretation:
Extreme imbalances (e.g. 80% longs) suggest contrarian trades may be profitable.
Timeframe: Short-term (hours to a few days).
Data Source: CoinGlass, Binance API, Coinalyze.
See:
Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle1\Binance_summary_statistics_BTCUSDT_premium.csv (half the indicator)


5. Spot vs. Perpetual Price Basis (Funding Premium)
Definition: Difference between spot BTC price and perpetual contract mark price.
Interpretation:
Positive premium → market is over-leveraged long.
Negative premium → market is overly short.
Reversion of the basis often precedes price reversals.
Data Source: Deribit, Binance, CoinMetrics.
See:
Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle2\BTC_Binance_BasisSpotFutDifference.csv  (proxy)

6. BTC Dominance (% of total crypto market cap)
Definition: BTC market cap relative to altcoin market cap.
Interpretation:
Rising dominance: risk-off behavior (short-term flight to BTC).
Falling dominance: risk-on altcoin rotation.
Timeframe: 1–5 days.
Data Source: TradingView, CoinMarketCap.
See:
Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle1\BTC_mktcap_valuation.csv (bitcoin half of the indicator)


7. Realized vs. Implied Volatility
Definition:
Realized volatility: backward-looking actual price variability (=standard deviation of BTC price)
Implied volatility (IV): market-implied expectation from options pricing (=standard deviation of BTC options price)
Interpretation:
High IV vs. low RV → overpricing of risk, likely volatility crush.
Low IV vs. high RV → underpricing, risk of spike in implied volatility (potential for large move).
Timeframe: 1–7 days.
Data Source: Deribit, Laevitas, Skew (via Amberdata).
See:
Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle2\BTC_Deribit_DVOL.csv (implied volatility index)
Indicators\CryptoIndicators\CryptoDataBundle1\BTC_Volatility_DVOL_plus.csv (implied volatility index, more detail)

8. Net Taker Volume 
Definition:  the difference between buying and selling volume on futures contracts of Bitcoin (market orders = takers only, not limit orders).
Data Source: Binance, CryptoQuant
https://cryptoquant.com/community/dashboard/647f15832ec8802caabb3f42?utm_source=chatgpt.com
See: 
Indicators\CryptoIndicators\net_taker_volume_10y.xlsx

Summary 			Table
Indicator			Type		Lead Time	Best for
Funding Rate			Sentiment	<12h		Positioning extremes
Open Interest Change		Positioning	<24h		Trend continuation
Long/Short Ratio		Sentiment	<24h		Contrarian trades
Spot vs. Perpetual Basis	Structural	<12h		Mispricing reversion
BTC Dominance			Market Rotation	1–3d		Macro risk flows
Implied vs. Realized Vol	Volatility	1–5d		Options trade bias
Bitcoin Net Taker Volume        Sentiment       < 12–24 h       Directional bias / liquidity exhaustion / momentum shifts

If you're building a trading model or just manually interpreting signals, the combination of funding rate + OI changes + liquidations + basis tends to offer the most coherent view of short-term leveraged trader behavior.
